It's okay—this was just a test!

You recently clicked on a link in an email regarding "Crew Payroll & Payslips."

What happened? This was a simulated phishing exercise conducted by the Fleet IT Security Team. Had this been a real attack, your credentials or the ship's network could have been compromised.

How to spot a "Phish" next time:

1. Check the Sender

Always verify the email address. Real payroll notices will only come from our official company domain, not a public Gmail or slightly misspelled address.

2. Hover Before You Click

Hover your mouse over any button. If the address looks like a random string of numbers (IP address) or an unknown website, do not click.

3. Sense of Urgency

Phishing emails often threaten "delayed pay" or "account suspension" to force you to act quickly without thinking.

4. Unusual Timing

Be suspicious of administrative emails sent at unusual hours or outside of the standard monthly payroll cycle.

Safe Sailing starts with Cyber Security.
If you see a suspicious email, please report it to the Master or the IT Service Desk immediately.
